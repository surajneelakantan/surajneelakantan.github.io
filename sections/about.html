About

A PhD candidate in Computer Science at Örebro University. During the first few years of my PhD, I have been fortunate to collaborate with Orexplore AB — a company developing cutting edge machine for scanning geological samples. During that collaboration my research applies deep learning to the unique challenges of geological data: limited labels, and integration mutile geolological data sources. Outside this collaboaration, for my PhD thesis, I have also explored Human-in-the-Loop image super-resolution approach for improving interpretability and control in deep learning models. As the the field of deep learning continues to evolve, I am excited to explore new application areas.

My PhD thesis, Learning Beneath the Surface, presents a unified framework built around three core themes: domain adaptation, integration multiple geolological data sources and domain knowldge using LLMs, and interpretable image super-resolution for geoscience and remote sensing applications.

Beyond research, I co-developed the Neural Networks course at Örebro University and am passionate about sharing my experience of application of deep learning in geoscience with practioners/ geologists through a academic-industry course.

Outside research, I enjoy sports, be it watching or playing. A cricket nerd at heart, I also equally enjoy formula-1 and tennis. I am an avid reader with a particular fondness for non-fiction, science fiction, and crime novels. When it comes to music, psytrance is my go-to genre for its energetic beats and immersive soundscapes. Keeps me hooked on. That's all for now, but I'm always up for a chat about technology, science, books, politics or anything else that piques my curiosity!
