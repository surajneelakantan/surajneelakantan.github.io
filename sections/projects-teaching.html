SMARTER.AI — Intelligent Course Generation for Professional Learning
Örebro University · ARC Center

Learning is not one-size-fits-all. SMARTER.AI ss an intelligent course generation chatbot that builds personalized, constraint-aware learning journeys from a rich repository of courses from Örebro University.

How it works
The system automatically crawls and indexes course materials hosted on Nextcloud, classifying each resource — slides, videos, quizzes, readings — and enriching it with AI-generated metadata using LLaMA 3. At its core, a hybrid retrieval engine combines semantic search (Sentence-BERT embeddings) with lexical matching (TF-IDF) to surface the most relevant modules. A constraint-aware selection algorithm then optimizes the final course plan against your time budget, skill level, and preferred format.

The result: a personalized learning journey, whether you need a focused two-hour crash course or a structured multi-week deep dive.

White Blood Cell Analysis Using Deep Learning
Master's Thesis · Halmstad University · 2020

This project was carried out in collaboration with HemoCue AB, a global leader in point-of-care hematology testing, with the goal of developing a deep learning pipeline for their HemoCue® WBC DIFF System — a medical device that measures total white blood cell count and differential in minutes.

The system works with data captured by an autofocus camera that generates a stack of images per detected cell at varying focal lengths. The core challenge was to build CNN-based models capable of both identifying white blood cells (distinguishing single cells from doubles, triples, and debris) and differentiating between the major WBC types: neutrophils, lymphocytes, monocytes, and eosinophils.

Two classification strategies were explored — a standard multi-class CNN and a One-vs-All approach — alongside novel CNN architectures that leveraged multi-focal image stacks as multi-channel and multi-input networks. The final integrated model demonstrated strong performance across both identification and differential classification tasks, validated against a clinical reference analyzer and meeting industry-standard error margin requirements.
